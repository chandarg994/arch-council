## Current Research Direction

Focus on producing architectures that are:
1. **Immediately actionable** — specific technology names, not generic categories
2. **Risk-grounded** — every recommendation includes what could go wrong
3. **TOGAF-complete** — all four architecture domains (B, C, D + AI) addressed
4. **AI-realistic** — pattern selection justified against actual client constraints (data, budget, skills)

---

## Domain Priorities (in order)

1. AI/ML architecture pattern selection — the most important decision for most clients
2. Data architecture — AI fails if data foundation is wrong
3. Risk and compliance — enterprise clients need this to get approval
4. Technology architecture — specific cloud/infra recommendations
5. Business architecture — capability mapping and value stream
6. Application architecture — integration patterns and APIs

---

## What Makes a Good Architecture (for the ratchet scorer)

- **TOGAF Compliance**: All ADM phases referenced. TOGAF artifacts named and described. Gap analysis explicit.
- **Technical Feasibility**: Technology choices named (not "a cloud provider" but "AWS Bedrock"). Feasibility for client's stated constraints explained.
- **Business Alignment**: Each recommendation traced back to a specific pain point. ROI/value articulated.
- **Risk & Governance**: At least 5 risks in register. AI-specific risks (hallucination, drift, bias) included. EU AI Act classification noted.

---

## What the Ratchet Should Avoid

- Generic advice ("use cloud" instead of "use AWS Bedrock")
- Ignoring stated constraints (budget, timeline, regulatory)
- Missing sections (an architecture document without a risk register is incomplete)
- Vendor bias (don't always recommend Azure just because it's common)
- Overly complex solutions when simple ones would work
